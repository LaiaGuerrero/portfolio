a curated registration website for the Casa Herrera Seminar

THE CHALLENGE

How might we design a digital experience that unlocks an elegant, intuitive registration process and curated local insights for Casa Herrera Seminar’s international attendees?

The previous site had fallen short—unoptimized for mobile, uninspiring, and missing that touch of brand magic. With attendees ranging from beauty professionals to marketing leads—and most signing up on their phones—we needed to design an elegant yet practical experience that aligns with the prestige of Casa Herrera while addressing real user needs.

THE SOLUTION

The solution was driven by a core idea: turn registration into an experience. One that starts long before the seminar, and already reflects the quality and care of the event itself.

Three strategic design decisions brought this to life:

Mobile-first as a mindset

With over 68% of users registering via mobile, we prioritized clarity, speed, and beauty on small screens—ensuring that luxury could live comfortably in a thumb-scroll world.

Experience Barcelona

We transformed typical travel info into a swipeable, story-style section. Guests explored handpicked spots in Barcelona in a format that felt intuitive and inspiring.

A digital key concept

Upon registering, users received a personal digital key that unlocked content progressively, creating a sense of exclusivity and building excitement before the event.


DESIGN PROCESS

Designing a site for Casa Herrera meant more than just visuals—it meant crafting a user journey that felt intentional, luxurious, and human. My process was grounded in strategy, collaboration, and iteration:

1. Understand

Interviewed 20 previous and potential attendees. Their pain points—slow mobile forms, impersonal flow, and lack of local guidance—shaped the foundation of the design.

2. Define

I immersed myself in the brand, identified key emotional and functional touchpoints, and mapped out a content structure that aligned with both user expectations and business goals.

3. Ideate

Sketched out the ideal user journey, built personas, and prototyped in Figma with a focus on blending usability with aesthetic depth.

4. Iterate

Tested with 10 users across devices. With each round, we refined copy, structure, and interactions—ensuring clarity without sacrificing elegance.

FINAL DESIGN

The final design delivered more than a smooth registration flow—it told a story. The interface was responsive and visually aligned with the brand’s DNA, featuring micro-interactions, iconography, and transitions that elevated the experience. Accompanied by dev-ready handoff and documentation, the product was as polished behind the scenes as it looked on screen.

LEARNINGS

Elegance is nothing without function

Designing for beauty and usability at once was a real challenge—but it taught me that clarity, rhythm, and hierarchy are what let visual richness shine. Luxury doesn’t have to slow users down.

Olivia, Event attendee: The website felt just like the brand—refined, intuitive, and truly welcoming. It made the whole process feel like part of the experience.

Immersion starts before arrival

The ‘Experience Barcelona’ page showed me the value of emotionally connecting users early. What began as “extra content” became a standout feature that enriched the overall journey.

Ricard, CH Education Manager: This is the first time our event site has gone beyond logistics. It really reflects the spirit of Casa Herrera and gave our guests something memorable before they even arrived.

Good handoff is good design

Working closely with developers reminded me that design doesn’t end at the prototype. Clear documentation, shared language, and technical empathy ensured we stayed aligned—from concept to code.

Anna, Front-End Developer: Your design logic and documentation made implementation smooth and cohesive. It felt like we were building the same product from day one.
